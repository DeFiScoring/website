A **Terms of Service (ToS)** is the legal contract between you and your users. Since you are providing "scores" that might influence financial decisions, this document needs to be robust, specifically addressing data usage, "as-is" service delivery, and the fact that you aren't an investment advisor.

Here is a comprehensive ToS for **DeFiScoring.com**.

### Implementation Tips for a Pro Finish:

  * **Markdown Placement:** Save this as `_pages/terms.md`. Jekyll will automatically render it into your site's layout.
  * **The "Agreement" Checkbox:** If you add that "Connect Wallet" feature we discussed earlier, make sure the user clicks a box that says: *"I agree to the Terms of Service and Privacy Policy."* This makes these terms legally enforceable.
  * **Footer Link:** Add `[Terms of Service](/terms)` next to your `[Privacy Policy](/privacy)` in the footer.
  * **Witty Pro-Tip:** Don't just copy-paste and forget. In the DeFi world, being transparent about **Section 5 (Third-Party Links)** is huge. Users need to know that just because Uniswap has a "99/100" score on your site, you aren't responsible if the Uniswap frontend gets hijacked.
